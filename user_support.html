User Guide

Adding Registered Buildings

  • Log in to your account on the application using your credentials.
  • Navigate to the "My Buildings" or "Registered Buildings" section in the menu.
  • Click on the "Add Building" button to start adding a new building to your repository.
  • Select from the list of registered buildings if they are already available in the system.
  • Save the information to register the building in your account.

Receiving Alerts for Registered Buildings

Once you have added buildings to your repository, you will automatically receive alerts and notifications for any emergencies detected in those buildings. Alerts may include fire alarms, evacuation notices, security breaches, or any other critical incidents based on the monitoring system integrated with the application.

Ensure that your notification settings are enabled to receive alerts via email, SMS, push notifications, or in-app alerts.

Using the Application During an Emergency

Follow the instructions provided in the alert message or dashboard to stay safe and take appropriate measures based on the nature of the emergency (e.g., evacuate the building, seek shelter, contact emergency services).

Triggering Alerts in Registered Buildings

  • If you witness an emergency situation in one of your registered buildings and need to alert others, use the "Trigger Alert" feature available in the application.
  • Go to the "Trigger Alert" or "Emergency Response" section and select the building where the emergency is occurring from your registered buildings list.
  • Confirm the alert trigger, and the application will notify other registered users, security personnel, and emergency responders about the situation.

Best Practices for Using Alerts and Emergency Features

  • Keep your contact information and notification preferences updated in the application settings to ensure timely receipt of alerts.
  • Regularly review and familiarize yourself with emergency procedures, evacuation routes, and safety guidelines specific to each registered building.
  • Participate in training sessions or drills organized by building administrators to practice using the application during emergencies.
  • Report any issues, feedback, or suggestions related to the application's emergency features to the support team for improvements and enhancements.
